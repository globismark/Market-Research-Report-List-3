<p><p>Organic Marigold Oleoresin is extracted through two main methods: Solvent Extraction and Supercritical Fluid Extraction (SFE). Solvent Extraction involves using solvents like hexane to separate the oleoresin from the plant material. On the other hand, SFE utilizes supercritical fluids such as carbon dioxide to extract the oleoresin in a more environmentally friendly way. Both methods are commonly used in the market to obtain high-quality organic Marigold Oleoresin for various applications in the food, cosmetic, and pharmaceutical industries.</p></p>
