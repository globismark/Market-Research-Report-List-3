<p><p>Livestock surface disinfectants come in various types based on their active ingredients. Quaternary ammonium compounds and phenols work by disrupting the cell membranes of pathogens. Hypochlorite and halogens, such as bleach, are effective at killing a wide range of microorganisms. Oxidizing agents and aldehydes work by damaging proteins and nucleic acids in pathogens. Other types of disinfectants may include acids, alcohols, or biguanides. Each type has its own unique mechanism of action to effectively sanitize livestock surfaces and prevent the spread of infectious diseases.</p></p>
