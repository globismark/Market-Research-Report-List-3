<p><p>Automotive Electronic Power Steering (AEPS) is a technology that uses electric motors to assist in steering a vehicle. Steer-by-wire systems eliminate the mechanical connection between the steering wheel and the wheels, offering more flexibility and control to vehicle manufacturers. These innovations are expected to drive further growth and innovation in the AEPS market in the coming years.</p></p>

<p><strong>The Automotive Electronic Power Steering Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>C-EPS</li><li>P-EPS</li><li>R-EPS</li></ul></p>
<p><p>C-EPS, or Column-Electric Power Steering, is a type of power steering system where the electric motor is mounted on the steering column. P-EPS, or Pinion-Electric Power Steering, has the electric motor mounted directly on the steering rack. R-EPS, or Rack-Electric Power Steering, places the electric motor on the steering rack as well, but with a different design than P-EPS. Each type of EPS system offers unique benefits and drawbacks, catering to different vehicle designs and driving preferences in the automotive industry.</p></p>
