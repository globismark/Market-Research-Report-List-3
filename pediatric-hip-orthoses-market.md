<p><p>Pediatric hip orthoses are devices used to support and stabilize the hip joint in children. The fixed hip orthoses are rigid devices that limit movement of the hip joint, providing stability and support for children with posture or alignment issues. On the other hand, hip articulated orthoses have movable components that allow for controlled range of motion, making them suitable for children with hip dysplasia or recovering from hip surgery. Both types of orthoses play a crucial role in improving mobility and quality of life for pediatric patients with hip conditions.</p></p>
