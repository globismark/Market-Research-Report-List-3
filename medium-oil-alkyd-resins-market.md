<p><ul><li>Dry Alkyd Resins</li><li>Non-Dry Alkyd Resins</li><li>Half Dry Alkyd Resins</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Medium oil alkyd resins are commonly divided into three main types based on their drying characteristics: dry alkyd resins, non-dry alkyd resins, and half-dry alkyd resins. Dry alkyd resins form a solid film upon drying, making them suitable for applications requiring a hard finish. Non-dry alkyd resins do not fully dry and remain tacky, often used for adhesives and sealants. Half-dry alkyd resins have intermediate drying properties, offering a balance between hardness and flexibility in coatings and paints.</p></p>
