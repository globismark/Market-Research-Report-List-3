<p><p>Ultra Large Scale Integrated Circuits (ULSI ICs) are classified into two main types based on the technology used for the fabrication of the circuits - Thin Film ICs and Thick Film ICs. Thin Film ICs involve the deposition of thin layers of materials onto a substrate, allowing for precise control and miniaturization of components. In contrast, Thick Film ICs use a more cost-effective and simplified manufacturing process by depositing thicker layers of materials onto the substrate. Each type of ULSI IC offers unique advantages and applications in the market.</p></p>
