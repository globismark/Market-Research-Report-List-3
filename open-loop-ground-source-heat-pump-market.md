<p><p>An open-loop ground source heat pump is a type of geothermal heating and cooling system that utilizes groundwater as a heat source or sink. This system circulates water from a well or surface water source through a heat exchanger to transfer heat to or from the ground. Open-loop systems are typically more efficient than closed-loop systems, as they can take advantage of the relatively constant temperature of groundwater.</p><p>The open-loop ground source heat pump market is expected to grow at a significant pace in the coming years, with a projected CAGR of 14.8% during the forecast period. <p><p>Open-loop ground source heat pump systems come in three main market types: horizontal, vertical, and surface water. Horizontal systems involve burying pipes in shallow trenches, making them ideal for larger properties with plenty of available land. Vertical systems require drilling deep boreholes, making them suitable for smaller properties with limited space. Surface water systems extract heat from bodies of water such as lakes or rivers, providing an alternative for properties near water sources. Each type offers unique benefits depending on the property's size and location.</p></p>
