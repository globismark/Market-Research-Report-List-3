<p><p>Orthopedic trauma fixation products are categorized into internal fixation and external fixation types. Internal fixation includes plates, screws, nails, and other devices used to stabilize fractures internally. Plates are used to hold fractured bones together, screws are used to secure plates or directly into bones, nails are inserted into the medullary canal, and other devices include pins and wires. External fixation involves devices that are placed externally to stabilize fractures, such as fixators and frames.</p></p>
