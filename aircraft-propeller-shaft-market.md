<p><p>The Aircraft Propeller Shaft market offers various types such as tapered, splined, and flanged shafts. Tapered shafts gradually decrease in diameter, providing a secure connection for rotating parts. Splined shafts have ridges or teeth that fit into corresponding grooves, ensuring precise alignment and torque transmission. Flanged shafts have a widened end with bolt holes for easy attachment to other components. Each type caters to different requirements in aircraft propulsion systems, offering versatility and reliability for various applications.</p></p>
