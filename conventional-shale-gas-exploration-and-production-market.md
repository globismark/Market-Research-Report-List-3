<p><p>Conventional shale gas exploration and production market types include proppants, such as sand, ceramics, resin-coated sand, and others. Proppants are used to keep fractures in shale rocks open to allow gas to flow to the surface. Sand is a commonly used proppant due to its availability and cost-effectiveness. Ceramics are known for their high strength and durability, making them suitable for deeper wells. Resin-coated sand provides improved proppant transport and placement in the well. Other types of proppants offer unique properties and benefits for shale gas extraction.</p></p>
