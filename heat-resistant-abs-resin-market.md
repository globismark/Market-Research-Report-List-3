<p><p>Heat resistant ABS resin is widely used in both blow molding and injection molding markets. In blow molding, the resin is melted and formed into a hollow shape by blowing air into the mold. This process is commonly used for manufacturing bottles, containers, and automotive parts. In injection molding, the resin is melted and injected into a mold under high pressure to create solid products such as electronic housings, appliance parts, and automotive components. Both markets rely on heat resistant ABS resin for its durability, strength, and heat resistance properties.</p></p>
