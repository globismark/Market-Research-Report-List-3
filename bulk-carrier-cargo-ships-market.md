<p><p>Bulk carrier cargo ships come in various market types to cater to different transportation needs. Geared bulk carriers are equipped with cranes for loading and unloading heavy cargo. Combined carriers can carry both bulk and general cargo. Gearless carriers do not have onboard cranes and rely on shore facilities for loading and unloading. Self-dischargers are equipped with conveyor belts for efficient unloading. Lakers are bulk carriers that mainly operate in the Great Lakes region of North America. Each market type serves a specific purpose in the bulk carrier industry.</p></p>
