<p><p>Digital Polymerase Chain Reaction (dPCR) is a powerful technique used for precise quantification of nucleic acids. There are two main types of dPCR technology in the market: Droplet Digital PCR (ddPCR) and Chip-based Digital PCR. </p><p>In ddPCR, the sample is divided into thousands of droplets, and PCR is performed in each individual droplet. This allows for absolute quantification of the target nucleic acid. </p><p>Chip-based dPCR, on the other hand, utilizes microfluidic chips to partition the sample into individual reactions, enabling highly accurate quantification of nucleic acids.</p></p>
