<p><ul><li>Optical Sensor</li><li>Capacitive Sensor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Automotive Rain Sensing Wiper Systems use either optical sensors or capacitive sensors to detect the presence of rain on the windshield and automatically adjust the wiper speed accordingly. Optical sensors use light to detect water droplets, while capacitive sensors detect the change in capacitance on the windshield surface. Both types of sensors are commonly used in modern vehicles to provide a convenient and safe driving experience, by allowing the wipers to operate without the need for manual adjustments in response to changing weather conditions.</p></p>
